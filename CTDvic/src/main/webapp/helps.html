1. What Information is Available in the CTDvic?

Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) variants are potential tumor biomarker present in circulating fluids. At present, minimally invasive or non-invasive ctDNA mutation detection technology based on liquid biopsy is increasingly being valued in cancer diagnosis and treatment monitoring, with enormous potential for clinical application.

Here, we established the CTDvic database (http://bio-bigdata.hrbmu.edu.cn/CTDvic), which aimed to documenting the clinical function and application value of ctDNA variants.



2. What data are Included in the CTDvic?

The current version of CTDvic manually curates 4,797 published literatures until 8 May 2024. Experimentally supported variants related to clinical action were categorized into two groups. The Variant-Level group includes 82 diseases, 158 genes, 12 mutation types, and 865 major missense mutations, and the Gene-Level includes 58 diseases and 132 genes; While the Prediction-Level data acquired through calculating 38 cfDNA mutation spectrum includes 21 diseases, 2,305 tumor driver genes, and 23,012 gene hotspot mutations.

3.4.1 geMER

The geMER integrates somatic mutations and element genomic sequence, screens mutation enrichment regions in genomic elements based on the mutation set enrichment analysis (MSEA). Briefly, it mimics a walker moving along the genomic sequence of a given element while recording an accumulation score, whose change depends on mutation occurrence and frequency. The maximum variation of accumulation score indicates both location and degree of mutation clusters. 3.4.2 Upload files

In the tools module, users can upload mutation spectra (from either tissue or cfDNA) (required). It will calculate tumor driver genes and hotspot mutations through the geMER algorithm. If users also upload the patient's survival data (optional), the server could calculate the driver gene prognostic value.

Variant.txt (Fig 1) and Survival.txt (Fig 2) file format instruction:

1. The 'Tumor_Sample_Barcode' column as patient ID.

2. The 'Chromosome' column as the chromosome where the mutation is located.

3. The 'Start_Position' column as the starting position of the mutation on the genome.

4. The 'End_Position' column as the ending position of the mutation on the genome.

5. The 'Hugo_Symbol' column as gene symbol.

6. The 'Reference' column as the nucleotide sequence before the mutation, using '_' to indicate unknown bases.

7. The 'Alteration' column as the nucleotide sequence after the mutation, using '_' to indicate unknown bases.

8. The 'Mutation_type' column as the mutation type, using '_' to indicate unknown types. allowed types include:

'MISSENSE', 'EXON', 'FRAMESHIFT', 'NONSENSE', 'SILENT', 'PROTEINDEL', 'PROTEININS', 'SPLICE_REGION', 'SPLICE', 'INTRON', 'UTR_3', 'UTR_5', 'NONSTANDARD', 'NONCODING', 'SNV', 'MNV', 'insertion', 'Small_deletion'.

9. The 'patient_id' column as patient ID.

10. The 'status' column as the survival or disease progression status of the patient at the last follow-up.

11. The 'PFS' as progression-free survival, which can be replaced by 'OS' (overall survival).

3.7 Submit

The "Submit" page provides a strategy for sharing experiments validated variants data for CTDvic update. The submitter is required to fill in the relevant information for the variant, as well as the user's e-mail. Please use "-" instead of in the unknown option. Refer to the following example:

1. "PaperDoi" should be filled with the DOI number of the reference literature.

2. "DiseaseName" should be filled with the name of the disease related to the experimentally verified variant.

3. "Gene" should be filled with the gene symbol involved in the experimentally validated variant, such as TP53, KRAS, GNA11, etc.

4. "VariantType" should be filled with the type of the experimentally validated variant, such as Missense, methylation, indel, etc.

5. "VariantDescription" should be filled with a detailed description of the experimentally validated mutation, such as G12C, c.1789A>T, PAX3-NCOA1 fusion, etc.

6. "BodyFluid" should be filled with the type of body fluid used in the liquid biopsy, including plasma, urine, cerebrospinal fluid, pleural effusion, sputum, semen, etc.

7. "Experiment" should be filled with the experimental method or a summary of the experimental process.

8. "Function" should be filled with the clinical function (utility) related to the experimentally validated variant.

9. "SequenceMethod" should be filled with the sequencing technique used in the liquid biopsy, such as NGS, ddPCR, etc.

10. "Email" should be filled with the email address of the submitter.
